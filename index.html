An explicit counterexample to the Dixmier conjecture for the third Weyl algebra

Marcus Teller · July 21, 2026 · PDF · sympy certificate · Lean proofs
Prepared with substantial assistance from AI systems (Claude Fable 5, Anthropic; cross-checked with GPT-5.6-sol, OpenAI). All computational claims are machine-certified; see below.

1. What this is

Alpöge's counterexample to the Jacobian conjecture in dimension three (announced July 19, 2026) implies, through the classical implication $\mathrm{DC}_3 \Rightarrow \mathrm{JC}_3$, that the Dixmier conjecture fails for the third Weyl algebra $A_3$: there exists an injective, non-surjective $\mathbb{C}$-algebra endomorphism of $A_3$. The existence statement is immediate to experts. This note supplies what the observation does not: the explicit witness, which is (to our knowledge) the first explicit counterexample endomorphism of any Weyl algebra since Dixmier posed the problem in 1968.

With $u = 1 + x_1x_2$, Alpöge's map $F = (F_1, F_2, F_3)$ is

$$\begin{aligned} F_1 &= u^3x_3 + x_2^2\,u\,(4+3x_1x_2), \\ F_2 &= x_2 + 3x_1u^2x_3 + 3x_1x_2^2(4+3x_1x_2), \\ F_3 &= 2x_1 - 3x_1^2x_2 - x_1^3x_3, \end{aligned}$$

with $\det J = -2$ identically and the explicit collision $F(0,0,-\tfrac14) = F(1,-\tfrac32,\tfrac{13}{2}) = F(-1,\tfrac32,\tfrac{13}{2}) = (-\tfrac14, 0, 0)$. Set $N = -\tfrac12\,\mathrm{adj}\,J = J^{-1}$, a matrix of polynomials.

Theorem. The assignment $$\varphi(\hat x_i) = F_i(\hat x_1,\hat x_2,\hat x_3), \qquad \varphi(\partial_i) = \sum_{k=1}^{3} N_{ki}(\hat x_1,\hat x_2,\hat x_3)\,\partial_k$$ extends to a $\mathbb{C}$-algebra endomorphism $\varphi\colon A_3 \to A_3$ which is injective and not surjective. The Dixmier conjecture therefore fails for $A_n$ for every $n \ge 3$.

The lift $F \mapsto \varphi$ is classical (Bass–Connell–Wright p. 297, crediting Vaserstein and Kac; van den Essen Ch. 10; Bavula arXiv:2112.03177). What is new is the input: until this month the recipe had no non-invertible Keller map to apply it to. Because the lifted operators have order at most one, no quantum corrections arise, and well-definedness is equivalent to two families of polynomial identities ($JN = I_3$, and flatness $\sum_k (N_{ki}\partial_k N_{lj} - N_{kj}\partial_k N_{li}) = 0$). Non-surjectivity follows from maximal commutativity of $\mathbb{C}[\hat x]$ plus the collision. Full details and proofs are in the PDF.

As corollaries: an explicit counterexample to the Poisson conjecture $\mathrm{PC}_3$, and a symplectic Keller counterexample — the cotangent lift $\Phi(x,y) = (F(x),\, N(x)^{\mathsf T}y)$ on $\mathbb{C}^6$ preserves the canonical bracket, has $\det J\Phi = 1$ identically, and is not injective.

A Lean 4 / mathlib development proves the same identities plus the collision, with no sorry and no axioms beyond the standard three (propext, Classical.choice, Quot.sound): alpoge_keller ($\det J = -2$, entries certified as formal pderivs), alpoge_not_injective (the collision), dixmier_inverse/', dixmier_flatness (all 27 cases), det_adjugate_J, and poisson_det_scaled. 3. Remarks

$\mathrm{DC}_1$ and $\mathrm{DC}_2$ remain open, and $\mathrm{DC}_2 \Rightarrow \mathrm{JC}_2$: the two-variable Dixmier conjecture is at least as strong as the plane Jacobian conjecture, which survives. Zheglov (arXiv:2410.06959) proposes a proof of $\mathrm{DC}_1$. If $\mathrm{DC}_1$ holds, no endomorphism of $A_1$ has a plane Keller counterexample as its classical shadow, whereas in dimension three quantization succeeds with no corrections. Locating the obstruction that appears only in low dimension is, we believe, a fruitful new question about the plane Jacobian problem.
